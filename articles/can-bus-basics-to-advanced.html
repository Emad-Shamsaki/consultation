CAN Bus Basics to Advanced

A compact walkthrough of frames, arbitration, bit timing, filtering, and the debugging habits that help on real systems.

Emad Shamsaki
Sep 2, 2025 | CAN | 7 min read

CAN remains one of the most practical field buses in embedded systems because it combines reliability, robust arbitration, and a straightforward electrical layer. The complexity comes later, when timing margins, filters, or multi-node interactions are not validated carefully.

First layer: the frame

Before tuning bit timing, make sure everyone on the team can read one CAN frame correctly: identifier, control field, payload, CRC, acknowledgement, and inter-frame spacing.

Second layer: timing

Stable communication depends on correct baud rate, sample point, oscillator tolerance, cable length, and termination. Many field issues that look like software bugs turn out to be timing or physical-layer problems.

Simple bus-load estimate
Load = FramesPerSecond × BitsPerFrame BitRate

Third layer: debugging

  • Check bus termination before changing firmware.
  • Measure error counters when nodes enter error passive or bus off.
  • Verify acceptance filters before assuming a transmit issue.
  • Log timestamps when latency matters.
